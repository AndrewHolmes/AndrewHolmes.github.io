24 Hours of Lemons

The 24 Hours of Lemons is an endurance race event for $500 cars. Teams buy crappy cars, fix them up, install a roll cage, and take it to the track. Myself and five other Olin students bought a $250 1987 Mercedes 300E on Craigslist in the fall of 2016, committed to making it a race car. When purchased, the car had a blown head gasket, leaking coolant, horrible breaks, and more ciggarette butts than you could possibly imagine. Working out of a teammates garage in Portsmouth, New Hampshire, we set to work restoring it.

Over the course of the fall 2016 semester we managed to fix the engine and get the car running. We replaced broken fuel lines, fixed the blown head gasket, and patched the leaking coolant issues. YO DIS WAS COOL
